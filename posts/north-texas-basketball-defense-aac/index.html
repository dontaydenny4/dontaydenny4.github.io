The North Texas Men’s Basketball team has had one of the top defenses in their conference for the last few seasons. They recently joined the American Athletic Conference in which many people thought they would fall off. UNT just finished their second season in the AAC in which they finished 2nd in the conference behind the Memphis Tigers who earned a 5 seed in the NCAA Tournament in March.

UNT fans have seen time and time again how good defensively this Mean Green team can be over this season and the past few seasons. They were dominant in the Conference USA when Grant McCasland was at the helm leading the Eagles. North Texas was near the top in a few defensive categories in their first season last year in the American Athletic Conference, but the question is, were they the best this season defensively in the American Athletic Conference?

The first thing that we will look at in comparison with North Texas and the rest of the AAC teams is defensive efficiency. Defensive efficiency measures how well a team can stop or prevent the other team from being able to score per 100 possessions. You typically hope for the defensive efficiency to be the lowest.

North Texas has the best defensive efficiency in the entire American Athletic Conference which shows that their defense was the most efficient in their conference alone and the top defensive efficiency in the nation was Houston with 0.896, another team that used to be in the American Athletic Conference.

But where does UNT sit in terms of how many steals they get, blocks, and forced turnovers. Three important aspects of a good defensive team.

UNT ranks in the top four of the conference for forced turnovers and steals, but among the bottom of the conference in blocks.
